Mathematics

0606
Additional Mathematics
Cambridge IGCSE · 0606 · Single tier · June 2026 — highest priority subject
Syllabus topicHow the ladder project applies it
FunctionsP02 — EMA is a composite function: each value is a function of the previous. P06 — Logistic/sigmoid function f(x) = 1/(1+e⁻ˣ) graphed and applied.
Quadratic functionsP06 — The loss function (mean squared error) is quadratic. Minimising it requires finding the vertex — completing the square has a direct machine learning application.
Factors of polynomialsP08 — Polynomial fitting of price trend lines; remainder theorem appears when checking if a given period is a factor of the price cycle.
Equations & inequalitiesP07 — Backtesting entry/exit conditions coded as inequalities (e.g. enter if EMA_fast > EMA_slow). P10 — Risk constraints expressed as inequality systems.
Simultaneous equationsP06 — Solving for model weights during linear regression training is equivalent to solving a system of simultaneous equations (normal equations).
Logarithms & exponentialsP02 — EMA uses exponential smoothing; the decay constant is a direct application. P06 — Log-loss function uses natural logarithm — the first time ln appears in a real application.
Straight line graphsP06 — Linear regression baseline model (y = mx + c fitted to price data). P03 — Line of best fit on a correlation scatter plot.
Circular measureP08 — ARIMA seasonal components use radian-based periodic functions. Arc length and sector area reinforce the periodic structure of price cycles.
TrigonometryP08 — Fourier series applied to price data decomposes market movement into sin and cos harmonics. The double-angle and compound-angle formulae appear in the decomposition.
Permutations & combinationsP10 — Probability of exactly k consecutive wins in n trades uses ⁿCₖ. P05 — Combinations of news sentiment factors feeding into a classification score.
Binomial expansionP10 — Binomial distribution of trade outcomes (win/loss as Bernoulli trial). The expansion (p+q)ⁿ gives the probability distribution of n trades.
VectorsP11 — Feature vectors for K-Means clustering. Vector magnitude = distance from cluster centroid. P03 — Correlation matrix as a table of vector dot products.
MatricesP03 — Correlation matrix is a real matrix students compute and interpret. P06 — Matrix multiplication in model training (X·W = predictions).
DifferentiationP06 — Gradient descent is the derivative of the loss function. This is the single most important application: d/dx(loss) drives every neural network ever trained. P08 — Rate of change of volatility over time.
IntegrationP01 — Area under the equity curve (total return) is a definite integral. P08 — Cumulative distribution of returns uses integration under a probability density curve.
KinematicsP12 — The RL agent uses velocity (momentum) and acceleration (rate of momentum change) analogies. Displacement–time and velocity–time graphs mirror price–time charts directly.
0625
Physics
Cambridge IGCSE · 0625 · Extended tier · June 2026
Syllabus topicHow the ladder project applies it
Measurement & unitsP01 — Precision of pip values (4 decimal places), significant figures in return calculations, and unit consistency across data frames.
Motion, speed & velocityP02 — Price momentum is the market equivalent of velocity. Rate of change of price = velocity. P12 — RL agent modelled on particle in a potential field.
Forces & Newton's lawsP10 — Support and resistance in markets modelled as equilibrium: equal and opposite forces (buying pressure vs selling pressure). Newton's third law as a market metaphor.
Energy, work & powerP07 — Equity curve energy analogy: drawdown = loss of potential energy; recovery = work done by the system. Power = rate of return per unit time.
PressureP10 — Order flow pressure (volume of buy vs sell orders) maps to force per unit area. A qualitative but memorable conceptual bridge.
WavesP08 — Price oscillations modelled as waves. Frequency (how often a cycle repeats), amplitude (size of swing), period (time per cycle) all appear in ARIMA seasonal components.
Electromagnetic spectrumP05 — Signal-to-noise ratio is a direct physics concept applied to filtering genuine market signal from random news noise.
Electricity & circuitsP09 — Logic gates (AND, OR, NOT) in the rule-based chatbot's decision tree. Boolean logic in circuits = Boolean conditions in trading rules.
Digital electronicsP09 — Binary decisions (buy=1, sell=0) in trading logic map directly to digital logic and truth tables in the Computer Science / Physics overlap.
Experimental skills & dataAll projects — Every backtest follows the scientific method: hypothesis (strategy idea) → experiment (backtest) → measurement (metrics) → conclusion (deploy or discard). Physics laboratory skills transfer directly.

Skill areaHow the ladder project practises it

Unseen prose analysisP09 — Feed financial prose into the chatbot and analyse narrative voice, how language constructs authority, and how structure guides the reader — core unseen prose skills.

Language, form & structureP05 — How news articles use structure to create urgency: inverted pyramid, active verbs, short sentences. Analysing journalistic form is the same skill as analysing poetic form.
Context & meaningP12 — Historical context of financial crises (2008, hyperinflation in Zimbabwe) studied as literary context. Films like The Big Short treated as modern narrative texts.
Personal responseP04 — Weekly trading journal entries written in reflective first-person voice with opinion supported by evidence. The personal response essay, practiced daily.
Comparison of textsP05 — Compare two news articles covering the same economic event from different outlets. Same method as comparing two poems: shared theme, contrasting method, evaluative conclusion.
Imagery & metaphorP02 — Financial language is rich with metaphor: "the market is breathing," "support is holding," "bulls charging." Analyse these as literary devices before coding the indicators they describe.
